Data Pipeline

  • Filters by demo score + zoning pre-screen
  • Exports green-zoned CSVs to Zack
  • Automated cold outreach emails to listing brokers
  • Reply detection classifies responses (interested / not available / bounce)

Broker Network
  • Activates broker relationships — RTL gets market brief, local brokers send curated options
  • Browses LoopNet, Crexi, CommercialCafe directly
  • Cross-references broker leads against AV pipeline to avoid duplicates

Zack sometimes sees a listing before AV's data catches it, and vice versa

Too Many / Too Restrictive Filters
  • Once all current filters are applied, essentially all locations get eliminated
  • Even just demographics + zoning + play space takes 100s-1000s down to a handful
  • At top of funnel with the largest number of options, we should only remove listings that clearly fail the easiest-to-check criteria
Propose: Only filter on demographics, zoning, pricing, and neighborhood (including play space) at this stage. Already shown to reduce options to manageable levels, and all are fast or can be sped up.

Step 3 Candidate Location Analysis

Data Screening
  • Demographic scoring (enrollment + wealth)
  • Zoning confirmation (by-right vs. CUP vs. prohibited)
  • Outdoor space check (park/playground within 0.5mi)
  • Regulatory flags (airport proximity, railroad adjacency)
Passing addresses to Zack →
→
Site Screening + Judgment
  • 100-point AI screening (pickup/dropoff, parent perception, outdoor, building quality, adjacent risk)
  • Tier 1 kill checks (shared bathrooms, 3rd+ floor, bar sharing wall, airport)
  • Reviews GREEN/AMBER scores — applies SPOVs and gut check
  • Checks listing flyers for photos, SF, parking
  • First outreach call to broker on passing sites
  • Creates deal files for viable sites

100s-1000s enter. 0-5 survive per market.

Speed of Location Analysis
  • Analysis is split across dimensions with wildly different speeds — demographics, price, and zoning are fast; neighborhood, building, and regulatory are extremely slow (web-search dependent)
  • Inconsistency in completing all analyses when properties are ingested into the database
Propose: Eliminate building and regulatory checks here — those are uncovered in SIR from Ops later. Switch neighborhood checks from web-search to data-based. Final state: fast demographics, price, zoning, and neighborhood as the site selection filters.

Step 4 LOI Signing + Parallel Ops/Marketing Evaluation

Not involved at this stage

Primary
  • Negotiates LOI terms with broker/landlord (rent, lease length, break option, deposit, TI, escalations)
  • Drafts LOI on Alpha template, manages counter-proposals
  • Gets pre-approval from Neeraj/Andy on economics
  • On execution: triggers Ops (SIR), Marketing (2-week veto window), Matterport scan
  • P2/Ops reviews floorplans, estimates kid capacity + reno cost
Marketing Kill Criteria Unknown
  • Beyond standalone, play space, and drop-off/pick-up, we have no clear kill criteria from Marketing
  • Leads to recommending sites outside their desired cities/submarkets — sites get rejected late in process
  • The more feedback we get on why sites get rejected, the better we can tune upstream filters

Step 5 Site Visits & Lease Negotiations

Not involved at this stage

Primary
  • Routes lease to legal (SRD for standard, Dentons for complex)
  • Reviews every redline — decides which battles to fight
  • Site visit — walks neighborhood, checks pickup/dropoff, catches hidden issues
  • Coordinates insurance for non-standard coverages
  • Negotiates SIR cost allocation with landlord
  • Chases all parties to keep timeline from slipping

Target: 2 weeks. Reality: 4-6. Deals die from delay.

Non-Standard Leases Slow Everything
  • We have standardized LOIs, but landlords often want to use their own with non-standard terms
  • Worse for leases — almost always unable to use a standardized template
  • Landlords include unique/non-standard terms even when we use a template. More redline turns, slower process.
Handoff Management
  • Handoffs to other departments are not clean — leads to deal flow interruptions for ad hoc requests
  • Standardizing handoff timing is tricky due to complexity and fragile nature of deals
  • Too many people contacting the landlord during negotiations can disrupt things and kill deals

Step 6 Executive Sign-Off

Not involved at this stage

Primary
  • Presents deal to Andy/Neeraj — economics, ops estimate, risks, recommendation
  • Manages landlords calling daily while waiting for executive decision
  • Coordinates executive site visit (Neeraj, Andy, or Arthur)
  • On approval: DocuSign to Andy, wire transfer, COI from insurance
  • Builds complete ops handoff package
Decision Bottleneck
  • Leadership questions about a location's strategic position stall final decisions
  • Delays in getting feedback from all parties exacerbate the problem
Duplicated Site Visits
  • Zack visits first (feasibility + deal killers)
  • Ops authorizes Matterport + often walks the space themselves
  • Executive visit required before final sign-off
  • All represent duplicated effort — scheduling availability becomes a bottleneck to decision-making
